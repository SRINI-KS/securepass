Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about password security and our generator.

SecurePass uses the Web Crypto API's crypto.getRandomValues() function to generate cryptographically secure random numbers. This is the same technology used by banks and security-critical applications. We combine this with your selected character sets to create truly random, unpredictable passwords entirely on your device.

A strong password has three key characteristics: length (at least 12-16 characters), complexity (mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols), and uniqueness (never reused across accounts). Our generator ensures the first two, and we recommend a password manager for the third.

A truly strong password is practically unhackable through brute force. A 16-character password with all character types has about 10^30 possible combinations ‚Äì it would take billions of years to crack with current technology. However, passwords can still be compromised through phishing, data breaches, or malware, which is why 2FA is important.

Characters like 0/O, 1/l/I can be easily confused, especially when typing a password manually or reading it from a screen. Enabling this option makes passwords easier to transcribe without significantly reducing security. Keep it off for maximum randomness if you're copying/pasting.

Use a reputable password manager like Bitwarden, 1Password, LastPass, or your browser's built-in manager. These encrypt your passwords and sync them securely across devices. Never store passwords in plain text files, emails, or sticky notes.
